Who We are

We are making an effort to decrease food waste in homes.

We have seen groceries spoil because we did not know what to do with it. We were once not excited about the same home cooks meals week after week.

Our goal is to provide recipes that will excite you to cook again and be adventurous with your meals.

Households throw away about 30% of their groceries due to overbuying and poor planning.

Key Facts

  • Food goes to waste at every stage of food production and distribution - from farmers to packers and shippers, from manufacturers to retailers to our homes.
  • Households are responsible for the largest portion of all food waste - an estimated 76 billion pounds of food per year.
  • Between 30 and 40 percent of food in the United States goes uneaten - as much as 20 pounds of food per person per month are thrown away.
  • Many consumers misunderstand the purpose and meaning of the date labels that often appear on packaged foods. Confusion over date labeling accounts for an estimated 20 percent of consumer food waste.
  • Food spoilage and poor planning at home occurs due to partially used ingredients and misjudged food needs.

Those random leftover ingredients in your refrigerator tend to get forgotten, but they can come together to create some surprisingly delicious dishes, and reduce food waste at the same time.
